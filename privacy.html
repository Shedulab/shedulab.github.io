This Privacy Policy explains how Shedulab ("we", "us", or "our") collects, uses, and shares information when you use the Study Flow mobile application ("the App"). Study Flow turns your documents, notes, and recordings into study material such as summaries, flashcards, and quizzes.

Please read this Policy carefully. Where we rely on consent, including for personalized advertising or tracking, we request it separately. Using the App does not by itself constitute consent to personalized advertising or tracking.

1. Information we collect

Account information

When you create an account we collect your email address and the display name you choose. Authentication is handled by Supabase. If you sign in with Apple or Google, we receive a unique identifier and the email address associated with that sign-in (which may be a private relay address if you choose to hide your email).

Content you provide

To generate study material, we process the files and content you upload, including PDF and PPTX documents, pasted text, and audio recordings, along with the text extracted from them and the study sets generated from that content.

Study activity

We store your learning activity, such as quiz results, flashcard reviews, progress, and reminder preferences, so the App can track your progress and send the reminders you enable.

Subscription information

Purchases and subscriptions are processed by Apple or Google and managed through RevenueCat. We do not receive or store your full payment card details. We receive subscription status (for example, whether you have an active subscription) so we can unlock paid features.

Device and usage information

We may collect basic technical information such as device type, operating system version, app version, and diagnostic or crash data to keep the App working reliably. Diagnostic tools may also collect performance data, logs, screenshots, view hierarchy, and masked session replay data when needed to understand and fix crashes or errors.

Optional rewarded ads and reward information

The App offers an optional, user-initiated rewarded ad through Google AdMob. The advertising SDK is initialized and an ad is requested only after you choose Watch Ad. Where required, the Google User Messaging Platform ("UMP") privacy form and the iOS App Tracking Transparency ("ATT") prompt are presented before the first ad request. An ad is requested only if UMP indicates that ads may be requested.

When you request an ad, the Google Mobile Ads SDK, UMP, and participating advertising partners may collect or receive your IP address, which may be used to estimate general location; device and app information; device or advertising identifiers; advertising data such as ads shown; interactions such as taps and video views; crash, diagnostic, and performance data; and consent selections or other privacy signals. On iOS, the Identifier for Advertisers ("IDFA") is available only if you grant ATT permission.

To verify that an ad was completed and credit the correct account, we send Google an internal Study Flow user identifier and a reward-session verification token. These values do not include your email address or display name. Google sends our backend a signed verification callback containing transaction, ad-unit, reward, and timestamp information. We store the ad session and its status, Google's transaction identifier, points awarded, point balance, and redemption history to operate the rewards feature and prevent duplicate or fraudulent rewards.

We do not send your uploaded files, pasted text, recordings, generated study material, email address, or display name to Google AdMob for ad targeting.

2. How we use your information

  • To provide the App's core features and generate study material from your content.
  • To create and secure your account and authenticate you.
  • To process subscriptions and unlock paid functionality.
  • To schedule the local study reminders you have enabled.
  • To request, deliver, personalize where permitted, and measure optional rewarded ads.
  • To record and respect advertising consent and privacy signals.
  • To verify ad completion, award and redeem points, enforce usage limits, and prevent fraud or duplicate rewards.
  • To maintain, troubleshoot, and improve the App.
  • To comply with legal obligations and enforce our Terms of Use.

3. Legal bases for processing

Where laws such as the GDPR, UK GDPR, KVKK, or similar privacy laws apply, we process personal information based on one or more legal bases: to perform our contract with you and provide the App, with your consent where required, for our legitimate interests in operating, securing, and improving the App, and to comply with legal obligations.

For rewarded ads, we process ad-session and reward records to complete the reward transaction you request and for our legitimate interests in maintaining accurate point balances, preventing duplicate rewards, and detecting fraud. We rely on consent where required for personalized advertising, access to advertising identifiers, storage of advertising-related information on a device, or tracking across apps and websites. Points are awarded for completing an ad, never for granting advertising consent or ATT permission.

5. Data retention and deletion

We keep your information for as long as your account is active. You can delete your account at any time from Settings → Delete account in the App. Deleting your account permanently removes your account, uploaded content, and generated study material from our active systems, subject to limited retention required for legal, security, or backup purposes. Note that an active subscription is billed by Apple or Google and must be cancelled separately through your store account.

We keep ad-session, reward-transaction, point-ledger, and redemption records while your account is active and for as long as reasonably necessary to maintain an accurate balance, prevent duplicate or fraudulent rewards, resolve disputes, and comply with legal obligations. When an account deletion request is completed, we delete the related ad-session, reward-transaction, point-ledger, and redemption records from our active systems unless limited retention is required for legal, security, fraud-prevention, accounting, dispute-resolution, or backup purposes.

Advertising consent and ATT choices remain in effect until you change or reset them, or until the relevant platform or provider determines that a new choice is required. Google determines the retention periods for information it processes under its own policies. See Google's data retention practices.

You can also request account and data deletion by emailing info@shedulab.com from the email address associated with your account, or by otherwise providing enough information for us to verify and process your request.

Where we transfer information internationally, we take steps to ensure it remains protected in accordance with this Policy and applicable law, including by using appropriate contractual or technical safeguards where required.

We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us information, please contact us so we can remove it.

9. Your rights

Depending on where you live, including if privacy laws such as the GDPR, UK GDPR, KVKK, or similar laws apply to you, you may have the right to access, correct, export, or delete your personal information, or to object to or restrict certain processing. You can exercise many of these rights directly in the App, or by contacting us using the details below.

Advertising and tracking choices

Watching a rewarded ad is optional, and you can use the App's core features without watching one. Where UMP presents a choice, you may choose whether to allow personalized advertising. Refusing or withdrawing advertising consent does not reduce the number of points awarded for an ad you complete, although an ad may not always be available.

When UMP indicates that a privacy-options entry point is required, a Privacy options control appears in the App's Settings. You can use it to revisit and update the advertising choices managed through UMP.

On iOS, you can deny or later change ATT permission in Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking. If ATT permission is denied or withdrawn, the IDFA is not available for tracking. This choice does not restrict the App's core features and does not change the reward value for a completed ad. Google may still process limited information needed to deliver non-personalized or limited ads, measure performance, and prevent fraud.

To ask questions about your advertising choices, withdraw consent where applicable, or exercise rights available under privacy law, contact us using the details below.
